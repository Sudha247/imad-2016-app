John McCarthy

Father of Artificial Intelligence

He who refuses to do arithmetic is doomed to talk nonsense.

Here is a timeline of major events in his life

  • 1927 - Born in Boston, Massachusetts on September 4, 1927.
  • 1944 - was accepted into Caltech where he skipped the first two years owing to his aptitude and self-study in math
  • 1948 - gradated from Caltech with a B.S in math.
  • 1951 - Received his PhD in math from Princeton.
  • 1955 - Became an assistant professor at Dartmoth College.
  • 1956 - Organised the famous Dartmouth Conference and coined the term "Artificial Intelligence".
  • 1958 - Designed the LISP programming language based on Lambda Calculus. LISP later on influenced many functional programming languages including Haskell, Scala, Elixir.
  • 1959 - He went to Massachussets Institute of Technology (MIT) and co-founded Artificial Intelligence Lab along with Marvin Minsky(RIP)
  • 1962 - Left MIT and became a full-time professor at Stanford University.

Our ultimate objective is to make programs that learn from their experience as effectively as humans do. We shall... say that a program has common sense if it automatically deduces for itself a sufficient wide class of immediate consequences of anything it is told and what it already knows.

John McCarthy in "Programs with Common Sense (1958)"
